Cataract

What is Cataract?

Your eyes have a crystalline lens that is normally transparent and helps focus light on the retina, similar to a movie screen. This lens sits inside a thin capsular bag. With age, the lens undergoes changes that cause it to lose transparency and become opaque. This clouding of the lens is called cataract. The opacity prevents light from passing through properly, resulting in blurred vision.

How is it treated?

The standard treatment is cataract surgery, typically performed through phacoemulsification. Using an operating microscope, surgeons make a tiny incision near the cornea. A thin ultrasound probe is inserted into the capsular bag to break up and remove the cloudy lens using ultrasonic vibrations. After removing the cataract, an artificial intraocular lens (IOL) is implanted into the same capsular bag to restore focusing ability.

Our advanced cataract surgery replaces the cloudy lens with an artificial intraocular lens. With quick recovery time and personalized treatment plans, we help restore clear vision and improve quality of life.

Comprehensive Retina Care

The retina acts as the eye's photographic film, crucial for quality vision. Modern diagnostic and treatment methods allow effective management of retinal disorders.

Karunya Eye Hospital features a fully-equipped retinal department offering medical management for various retinal conditions. Our specialists provide the latest treatments including diabetic retinopathy management and macular degeneration therapy.

Diabetic Retinopathy Management

Causes

Develops due to deteriorating retinal blood vessels in diabetes patients. Nearly all diabetes patients show signs after 25 years, with severity linked to blood sugar control.

Treatment Options

Advanced Eye Injections

Anti-VEGF drugs (Lucentis, Eylea) and steroids administered via painless injections to reduce macular edema. Performed under topical anesthesia with minimal complications.

Laser Photocoagulation

Precise laser treatment to seal leaky vessels or shrink abnormal growths. Proven to significantly reduce vision loss risk, performed in-office with topical anesthesia.
