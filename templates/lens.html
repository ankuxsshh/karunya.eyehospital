Why Choose Contact Lenses?

Contact lenses are discreet, effective, and suitable for both cosmetic and therapeutic use. Whether for sports or daily wear, they offer exceptional clarity and freedom from frames.

Minimal Distortion

Experience fewer magnification effects and reduced peripheral and chromatic aberrations.

Enhanced Comfort

Better correction for keratoconus and irregular astigmatism, offering excellent visual acuity.

Aesthetic Appeal

Maintain a natural look without the distraction of eyeglass frames.

Safety for Athletes

Perfect for active individuals who want clear vision without the risk of broken glasses.

Surgical Protection
  • • Post-operative corneal care

Contraindications for Contact Lens Use

While contact lenses are safe for many, certain conditions may make their use inappropriate or uncomfortable.

Dry Eyes or Lid Conditions

Conditions such as active blepharitis, styes, chalazia, or entropion may prevent safe lens wear.

Eye Infections

Avoid lenses during active infections such as conjunctivitis, corneal abrasions, or uveitis.

Systemic & Environmental Factors

Allergies, uncontrolled diabetes, pregnancy, and pterygium are relative contraindications for lens use.

Caring for Your Contact Lenses and Eyes

Proper care is essential for maintaining your contact lenses and eye health.

Wash Hands

Always wash and rinse your hands thoroughly before handling lenses.

Dry Your Hands

Dry your hands with a clean towel to avoid contamination.

Keep Nails Short

Short nails prevent injury while handling your lenses.

Apply Makeup After Insertion

Avoid applying makeup before inserting your lenses.

Clean Lenses

Clean your lenses before insertion and after removal.

Replace Solution

Replace the contact lens solution daily to maintain cleanliness.
